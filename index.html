The Untitled Poem | 未名的詩

A 5-minute short feature film inspired by the poem Fragments (《断章》) by 卞之琳 Bian Zhilin.

The poem is written like this in Chinese:
斷章 你站在橋上看風景
看風景人在樓上看你
明月裝飾了你的窗子
你裝飾了別人的夢

The following English version is translated by William Wang
As you are enjoying the scenery on a bridge
Upstairs on a tower people are watching you
The bright moon adorns your window
But you adorn others' dream

Between the poetic lines, what this poem stresses is the changing of perspectives between "you", "people", "the moon", "window", "you", and the tranquil and peaceful vibe in the scene. In this short, I was trying to imitate the similar delicate feeling of the tension through switching perspectives.

The script, storyboard, shooting, post-editing, ambient sound design are done by me. Music pieces used in the film are under the public domain.

The Kennedy Town

2019

It is interesting to do an experimental artwork in the special situation in Hong Kong. In September 2019, I completed a work with people mainly living in the Belcher Bay Park, a park located across Praya, Kennedy Town in Hong Kong.

I decided to go there when I’m free, for an undetermined but no longer than 21 number of days. I waited for men and women who are over 40, whom I didn’t know, to get into the park. I asked certain of them to take me somewhere of their choice, whatever they wanted, in Hong Kong. I took a picture of them in this environment which has special significance in their lives. I wrote a text describing the situation. From Sep.14th to Sep.22nd, 8 people participated. The pictures and commentaries below are an account of what happened.
