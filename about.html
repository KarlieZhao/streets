Karlie Zhao | 趙文然

I'm currently an undergraduate student at School of Creative Media, City University of Hong Kong. At SCM, I'm very fortunate to have experiences with multiple aspects of art, with people who came from diverse background. And my interests cover a range of interdisciplinary areas, including generative art, noise and sound study, procedural animation, film study, live coding and dancing.

With a solid programming background, I believe that I can bring the workshop and performances closer to the digital world. Interesting and relatively new techniques or perspectives of sound like live coding performances, sound synthesizing and visualization can be introduced to our workshops. Also, I think it will be cool if we can combine visual with music/noise performances and improvisations. I have been learning dancing for more than 15 years, and have performed on plenty of different stages.

In my point of view, one key element of a performance, no matter of what kind, is the performers. Their body languages, the tension of their expressions, and the communication among them and the audience can make a huge impact. And even in a digital music performance where the performer/artist doesn't need to be present on the stage, the appearance of a human on the stage add some colour, some interactivity, and a bit more fun to the performance, no doubt.

What do I expect from the workshop?

I am now learning a little bit of live coding sound by myself, which I found very inspiring and involves strong communication with the audience. I love this idea of people write computer programmes as part of their performing art, and would love to learn more about the basics of acoustic art and music theory to create better performances.

Also, I'm looking forward to experimenting with music/sound-based performing art with human performer involved. I expect that my background in dancing and performing can bring some surprises and fun to every participant!
